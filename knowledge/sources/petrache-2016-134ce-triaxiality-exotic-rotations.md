# Petrache et al. (2016): Triaxiality and exotic rotations in 134Ce

## Bibliographic Record

C. M. Petrache et al., *Physical Review C* **93**, 064305 (2016), DOI `10.1103/PhysRevC.93.064305`. The BibTeX key is uniquely matched as `petrache_2016_Triaxialityexotic`.

## Scientific Question and Motivation

The experiment investigates whether the high-spin structures of `134Ce` can be organized as coexisting triaxial, highly deformed and superdeformed configurations. It also tests earlier assignments of dipole bands and uses `134Ce` as a neighboring core for the chiral `133Ce` and wobbling `135Pr` region.

## Scope and Reading Depth

Full 18-page article read. Covered: reaction and Gammasphere setup, coincidence analysis, extended level scheme, angular-distribution and two-point anisotropy assignments, D1-D4 dipole bands, quadrupole/high-spin sequences, single-particle alignments, CNS configuration comparisons and the summary. Not covered: raw detector calibration files or independent reanalysis of every table entry.

## Summary

The Gammasphere study extends `134Ce` high-spin spectroscopy and combines angular-distribution/Rac assignments with CNS calculations. It supports a coexistence picture of triaxial, highly deformed and superdeformed structures, while leaving individual configuration assignments model conditioned.

## Experimental Conditions and Analysis

High-spin states were populated in two separate ATLAS runs of the 116Cd(22Ne,4n) reaction with a 112-MeV beam. The first run used a sandwich target consisting of a 50 microgram/cm2 Al front layer, a 1.48 mg/cm2 isotopically enriched 116Cd foil and a 150 microgram/cm2 Au backing. The second used a 1.48 mg/cm2 116Cd layer of the same enrichment evaporated onto a 55 microgram/cm2 Au foil. Gammasphere had 101 active Compton-suppressed HPGe detectors in the first run and 88 in the second. Events were unfolded into three- and four-dimensional gamma-energy histograms and analyzed with RADWARE. Spin/parity and multipolarities used angular distributions, and weak transitions used two-point angular-correlation anisotropy ratios Rac (PDF p. 2, Sec. II, Experimental Details).

## Analytical Reconstruction

The experimental chain is: high-fold Gammasphere coincidences -> level-scheme extension -> angular-distribution/Rac multipolarity and spin constraints -> linking and signature structure -> CNS comparison of energies, alignments and deformation. The shape and rotation-axis conclusions are therefore joint experiment-plus-model interpretations. In particular, a near-degenerate dipole pair or an alignment upbend is not by itself a wobbling or chiral observation.

## Competing Interpretations and Limitations

- Weak transitions and two-point Rac values can have large uncertainty; D4 assignments remain tentative.
- CNS configuration, deformation and rotation-axis labels are calculated interpretations, not direct measurements.
- The global coexistence picture should not be converted into a wobbling or chiral proof for any single band.

## Assumptions, Limits and Reverse Tests

- Weak lines use two-point Rac ratios and can have large uncertainties; tentative D4 assignments and any line absent in the present data must remain qualified.
- CNS configuration labels summarize dominant Nilsson amplitudes and do not directly measure beta, gamma or a rotation-axis vector. TAC or projected-shell-model calculations provide useful reverse tests.
- D1's positive parity relies on the observed E2/M1-E2 links and the absence/reinterpretation of the prior E1 candidate; this should be checked against Table I and spectra before paper use.
- The global triaxial-coexistence conclusion is stronger than any single band assignment but still depends on the model comparison and on the assumed rotating-liquid-drop reference.
