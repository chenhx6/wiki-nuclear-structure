# Mullins et al. (1993): Perturbed alignments in 141Gd

## Bibliographic Record

S. M. Mullins et al., *Physical Review C* **47**, R2447-R2451 (1993), DOI `10.1103/PhysRevC.47.R2447`. The BibTeX key is uniquely matched as `mullins_1993_Perturbedalignments`.

## Scientific Question and Motivation

The experiment asks how an i13/2 neutron intruder band behaves in the intermediate-mass `141Gd` region between the A approximately 135 and A approximately 150 high-spin regions. The focus is on intruder-band identification, alignment, dynamic moments of inertia and the interaction of crossing bands, not on wobbling.

## Scope and Reading Depth

Full five-page Rapid Communication read, including both reaction setups, charged-particle/gamma selection, level scheme, DCO and alignment analysis, band-1 assignment, CSM/TRS comparison and conclusions. Not covered: later remeasurements or modern lifetime measurements.

## Summary

The early `141Gd` in-beam study establishes an i13/2 intruder-band and alignment-analysis precedent. DCO, dynamic moments and CSM/TRS comparisons constrain the band assignment and crossing interaction, but the paper contains no wobbling claim.

## Experimental Conditions and Analysis

`141Gd` was populated with 112Sn(32S,2pn) at 155 MeV and 112Sn(33S,2p2n) at 170 MeV. The first experiment used the 8pi spectrometer with 20 Compton-suppressed HPGe detectors, a 70-element BGO calorimeter and the ALF miniball of 24 CsI(Tl) detectors; the second increased angular momentum without the charged-particle array. Charged-particle-gamma coincidences, fold/sum-energy gates and background-subtracted spectra selected the exit channel. DCO matrices at 37 and 79 degrees supplied directional-correlation ratios; alignment was calibrated from known E2-E2 pairs in `140Eu` and `143Sm` (PDF pp. 1-3).

## Analytical Reconstruction

The evidence chain is reaction-selected coincidences -> decay scheme and DCO constraints -> stretched-E2 band sequence and alignment -> intruder assignment -> CSM/TRS comparison of crossings. The paper demonstrates how alignment perturbations and interaction strength can be inferred from band observables, while also showing that a smooth upbend need not equal the sharp backbend predicted by a simplified crossing calculation.

## Competing Interpretations and Limitations

- DCO calibration and alignment transfer are reaction- and detector-specific, not universal heavy-ion prescriptions.
- The 602-keV multipolarity and i13/2 assignment combine multiple observables and model comparison; neither follows from one ratio alone.
- CSM omits the residual interaction and possible octupole correlations invoked for the gradual alignment.

## Assumptions, Limits and Reverse Tests

- DCO calibration uses known E2-E2 pairs from neighboring nuclei and detector-angle-specific calculations; the alignment transfer should not be generalized to every heavy-ion reaction.
- The assignment of 602-keV multipolarity and the orbital identity of band 1 depend on decay-out evidence and model comparison; they are not determined by a single DCO ratio.
- CSM/TRS calculations omit the residual interaction and possible octupole correlations invoked to explain the gradual alignment. A reverse test is a configuration-mixed cranking calculation with explicit interaction strength.
- This paper is an alignment/method source, not a wobbling or chirality source.
